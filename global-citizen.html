The Global Citizen Podcast

Conversations on global governance, sustainability, and social change produced by Katoikos.world.

Overview

The Global Citizen was a podcast series that explored global governance, international politics, sustainability, and social change through conversations with practitioners, policymakers, scholars, and civil society leaders.

Through interviews and discussions, the series aimed to make complex global issues more accessible while highlighting the role of citizens, civil society organizations, and international institutions in shaping global governance.

Background and Context

The podcast emerged as an extension of the Katoikos.world online magazine. While the magazine published analysis, commentary, and opinion pieces on international affairs, the podcast created a conversational platform where ideas could be explored through dialogue with individuals working on global challenges.

Episodes featured guests from diverse professional backgrounds, including former United Nations officials, researchers, activists, policymakers, artists, and civil society leaders. Discussions covered themes such as geopolitics, climate diplomacy, democratic governance, grassroots activism, multilateralism, and cultural approaches to peace and cooperation.

By combining expert insight with accessible dialogue, the podcast complemented Katoikos.world's broader mission of fostering informed discussion on global governance and citizenship.

The Initiative

The Global Citizen podcast consisted of recorded interviews focusing on contemporary global issues and governance challenges. Episodes were published on the Katoikos.world website and distributed through online podcast platforms.

Each episode typically featured a conversation between the host and a guest sharing insights from their professional work or activism. Topics ranged from international relations and climate policy to storytelling, civic engagement, and innovative approaches to social change.
