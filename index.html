Our origins and focus

Established in Brussels in 2013, the Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability (FOGGS) was a think-and-do tank that served as a research and ideas-generation centre, discussion forum, and advocacy mechanism. FOGGS was a Public Benefit Foundation under Belgian law and was governed by its statutes, as modified in 2018, 2020, and 2021.

The founders were inspired by the UN Secretary-General’s High-Level Panel on Global Sustainability (2010–2012), which helped introduce the concept of Sustainable Development Goals. The initial aspiration was to continue the Panel’s work, including on the economy. In 2018 the work of FOGGS refocused on developing a new “Grand Narrative” for globalisation.

You can find a non-exhaustive presentation of what FOGGS did here.

Our mission

  • Developed and promoted a Grand Narrative of hope for a people-centred, planet-friendly, inclusive, and sustainable globalisation in a digital world.
  • Helped address major global challenges through a revamped global governance system and engaged, responsible, and informed global citizens.
  • Sought to ensure rapid and transformative technological and digital advances contributed to a more just and equitable world, improving life for all people.

See also the statement issued on 24 January 2020 by the FOGGS Executive Board: “Securing the future of our species: Towards a global governance system able to manage an inclusive and just transition.”
