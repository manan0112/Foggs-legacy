Peace Reflection Group of Former UN System Staff (PRG)

Former UN staff reflecting on global challenges and advancing ideas to strengthen multilateral cooperation.

Overview

The Peace Reflection Group of former UN system staff (PRG) was an informal network of former international civil servants who drew on their collective UN experience to reflect on global challenges and contribute ideas for stronger multilateral cooperation. The group articulated common positions and recommendations to inform policy discussions and encourage constructive engagement by leaders and decision-makers.

PRG members stressed that while global challenges have intensified, the principles of the UN Charter remain fundamentally relevant; their work explored how to apply those principles more effectively today.

Background and Context

Members brought extensive experience from across the UN system and engaged in dialogue on global politics, economics, and governance. Guided by the UN Charter, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and widely shared ethical norms, the group examined how to strengthen international institutions and apply Charter principles through expanded international law and more effective enforcement mechanisms.

The Peace Reflection Group

PRG operated as a collaborative forum for former UN officials to exchange views and reflect collectively on global affairs. Objectives included:

  • Exchanging perspectives on developments in global politics, economics, and governance.
  • Exploring ways to strengthen the UN and global governance to advance the UN Charter, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and the 2030 Agenda.
  • Informing public debate through letters, articles, and other communications.
  • Developing and promoting initiatives on peace and security, sustainable development, humanitarian affairs, and human rights.
  • Building partnerships with experts, activists, and organizations for shared initiatives.

Activities

PRG activities included policy reflection, analytical papers, opinion pieces, and communications with international institutions and political leaders. Members developed shared positions on multilateralism, peacebuilding, and governance reform, issuing open letters, statements, and appeals, and convening discussions on emerging global governance challenges.

Publications or Research Outputs

PRG participants contributed publications and reflection papers on peace, security, and governance, including:

  • European Security in the Age of Trump and Putin (2025)
  • World Peace: Reclaiming the United Nations and Its Ideals (2025)
  • Resolving the Israel-Palestine Conundrum – A New Era (2024)
  • Treating Violence as a Pandemic Disease (2024)
  • Nuclear Facilities as Military Targets – The Zaporizhzhia Case (2023)
  • An Enhanced Role for the UN in Peace & Human Security (2023)
  • New Agenda for Peace (2023)

Participants also contributed blog posts and commentary on global governance, globalization, and human rights.

Outcomes or Impact

Through reflections, publications, and public interventions, PRG contributed to discussions on strengthening multilateralism and enhancing the role of international institutions in addressing global challenges. The group promoted effective application of the UN Charter’s principles, encouraged dialogue among policymakers and experts, and supported efforts to prevent conflict, advance sustainable development, and protect human rights.
