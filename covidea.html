COVIDEA (COVID Education Alliance)

An alliance to help education systems adapt to a rapidly changing, digital world in the wake of COVID-19.

Overview

The COVID Education Alliance (COVIDEA) was an international initiative to help education systems adapt to rapid digital change. Launched during the pandemic, it convened education specialists, technology experts, and policy practitioners to surface digital tools, curricula, and options that strengthen resilience and advance SDG 4.

Background and Context

COVID-19 school closures exposed structural weaknesses: limited digital readiness, unequal access, and gaps in remote learning capacity. Simultaneously, accelerating digital technologies, AI, and robotics raised questions about whether education systems were equipping learners for a tech-driven world. COVIDEA responded by exploring how digital solutions could bolster access, quality, and preparedness.

The Initiative

Co-convened by the Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability (FOGGS) and the Platform for Transformative Technologies (P4TT), with support from the International Science Council (ISC), COVIDEA aimed to gather digital educational tools, curricula, and technologies “under one roof.” It prioritized options aligned with SDG 4 and screened for bias, equity, and human rights considerations.

The initiative emphasized competencies for the future—critical thinking, adaptability, collaboration, self-awareness, and resilience—and sought frameworks to help policymakers and educators identify context-appropriate digital resources.

Activities

  • Outlined a platform to identify and showcase digital learning tools, curricula, and technologies relevant to modern education systems.
  • Explored criteria for assessing educational technologies to guide decision-makers in diverse contexts.
  • Promoted approaches that pair digital literacy with critical thinking, judgment, resilience, and responsible citizenship.
  • Encouraged collaboration among education communities and technology experts on innovative pedagogies and tools, including AI, VR/AR, simulation, and personalized learning.

Partners

Events and Dialogues

Enterprising Education for Fit-for-Future Results | COVIDEA and Parents International — 26 January 2022 (LearningPlanet Festival)

Plenary session on entrepreneurial thinking in re-imagining education systems. Veerle Vandeweerd and Georgios Kostakos introduced the COVIDEA approach; Eszter Salamon presented the “ParENTrepreneurial” model highlighting parents as equal partners in education system renewal.

Outcomes or Impact

COVIDEA advanced dialogue on adapting education to pandemic disruptions and digital transformation—emphasizing equitable access to digital tools, stronger digital literacy, and embedding critical competencies such as resilience and critical thinking. By surfacing technologies and frameworks, it supported policymakers and educators aiming to prepare learners for a rapidly changing, digital world.
