Project CASA / M4CE

Climate and Security Action through Civil–Military Cooperation in Climate-Related Emergencies.

Overview

Project CASA builds on earlier work conducted by the Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability (FOGGS) under the Militaries for Civil(ian) Emergencies (M4CE) project.

M4CE explored how military personnel, equipment, and logistical capacities support civilian authorities during disasters—from floods and wildfires to pandemics, hurricanes, earthquakes, and industrial accidents. It examined legal frameworks, operational practices, and policy implications of civil–military cooperation, identifying good practices and raising awareness of how military resources can complement civilian response systems.

That research laid the foundation for Project CASA, which expands the focus to climate-related emergencies and broader questions of climate security.

Background and Context

The scale and frequency of disasters are rising, putting pressure on emergency response systems. Extreme weather—floods, wildfires, hurricanes, droughts—and global crises like COVID-19 expose vulnerabilities in civilian infrastructure and response capacity.

When civilian capabilities are overwhelmed, authorities often turn to armed forces for logistics, engineering, transport, and trained personnel. This raises policy questions on legal authority, command and coordination, funding, and the balance of civilian and military roles.

M4CE examined these issues through comparative research and expert dialogue, including the coordination roles of the United Nations and NATO.

The M4CE Project

M4CE was initiated by FOGGS to understand the increasing use of military resources for non-military threats. Support was provided by the NATO Public Diplomacy Division.

The project aimed to highlight the role of military assets in disasters, examine legal and institutional frameworks, identify good practices, and stimulate policy discussions to improve response systems.

Work strands included desktop research and a background paper on military use in disaster response, interviews with practitioners and experts, and a public online discussion bringing together stakeholders to examine policy issues.

Publications and Research Outputs

Background Paper

Use of Military Resources to Address Natural and Human-Made Disasters — September 2021

Analyses how military assets support disaster response, reviewing legal frameworks and operational practices across France, Germany, Greece, India, Poland, Russia, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Explores logistics, medical services, engineering, evacuation, infrastructure support, and international coordination, including UN and NATO roles, as militaries are increasingly called to assist civilian authorities.

Events and Dialogues

Online Discussion — Militaries in Responding to Civilian Emergencies (M4CE) — 23 November 2021

Gathered experts and practitioners to examine legal frameworks for civil–military cooperation, command and coordination between civilian and military authorities, budgetary and operational considerations, training and preparedness, and cross-border cooperation and international coordination mechanisms.

From M4CE to Project CASA

Insights from M4CE informed subsequent research on climate change, security, and disaster response. Project CASA (Climate and Security Action through Civil–Military Cooperation in Climate-Related Emergencies) broadened the analysis to how militaries and civilian authorities cooperate specifically on climate-related emergencies.

Through research, dialogue, and collaboration among experts and institutions, the initiatives contributed to discussions on how civil–military cooperation can strengthen preparedness and response to complex emergencies in the twenty-first century.
