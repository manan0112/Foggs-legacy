FOGGS Update: July-August 2023

Upcoming FOGGS Event in New York
The global governance system, centered around the UN, is seen as a vital tool for addressing today’s global challenges, including climate change, pandemics and increasing economic inequalities. Proposals for reforming the system have been put forward by various parties, including the UN Secretary-General and civil society. FOGGS and partners from around the world are contributing with two significant inputs, namely the "Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform Report," presenting voices from developing countries, and the "PRG Booklet on an Enhanced Role for the UN in Peace and Human Security," offering critical insights from a former UN system staff perspective. These inputs aim to foster a more inclusive and effective UN for the future. In addition to launching and debating these two reports, the event will include two panel discussions, on the need for a Global Peace Architecture and a Global Resilience Council respectively.

The Agenda of the event and the registration form can be found here:

FOGGS celebrates ten years of existence
Officially established in August 2013, FOGGS is celebrating 10 years of existence by intensifying its work for a fair and inclusive globalisation, with people-centred and ethical global governance, sustainable development, resilience and peace.
A Call for UN Charter Reform
FOGGS, supports an initiative led by the Global Governance Forum that calls for UN Charter reform to break free from outdated governance structures and establish a just, principled, representative and accountable global order reflective of the needs of humanity and the planet at this time.
Join the call here and read the full reform statement here.
UNRISD podcast with FOGGS Executive Director
Georgios Kostakos speaks to Paul Ladd, the Director of the UN Research Institute for Social Development (UNRISD), about various crises that have eroded public trust in multilateral institutions, including the UN system. They discuss how multilateral institutions have fallen short with regard to global challenges such as the Covid-19 pandemic and how shifts in the global distribution of power call for an ethical approach and courage to renew multilateralism and rebuild trust. The conversation highlights ongoing efforts to reshape global governance and foster a more resilient and collaborative approach to address pressing global issues.

Targets designed in the 17 Sustainable Development Goals will not be achieved in communities such as Pós-Balsa in Brazil
by Louis Edoa, 1 August 2023
In October 2016, with the signing by the President of the Republic of Decree No. 8.892 of October 27, 2016, Brazil officially adopted the 2030 Agenda together with other UN member states, with the aim of achieving the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which cover social, economic, and environmental objectives for a better life for all. SDG fulfillment depends on actions by governments at all levels, civil society, the private sector, and all citizens. However, while it is clear that some communities and locations in the world will reach most if not all of the SDGs by 2030, others will continue to live in precarious situations. Disability matters for Climate Justice
by Renata Juliotti, 1 July 2023
The effects of climate change usually fall heavily on marginalized populations, including millions of persons with disabilities (PWDs) worldwide, especially those living in rural areas and poor communities. Almost 13 years ago, at COP16 of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), the 2010 Cancun Agreements identified PWDs as disproportionally affected by the climate crisis and emphasized inclusion as an essential element of climate justice. Sadly, climate change professionals and policymakers tend to overlook these individuals more than any other demographic group...
