Katoikos Editorial on Gaza
Gaza’s tragedy deepens as civilians, doctors, and journalists are killed under relentless bombardment. Despite crocodile tears and empty assurances, the red line of humanity is repeatedly crossed and the blood thickens. Our editorial calls for urgent action: UN expulsion of Israel, arms embargoes, unrestricted aid access, recognition of Palestine, and suspension of EU-Israel agreements. Civil society keeps the spotlight on Gaza—but governments must act now, before humanity itself erodes.

Education for Democracy | Project Democrat Conference in Barcelona, 20-21 November 2025

On November 20–21, 2025, the EU-funded Democrat Project will host a Conference on Education for Democracy at the University of Barcelona. The Democrat Project and its sister projects AECED and Critical Change Lab will use the occasion to present their findings on how civic and democratic education can strengthen the EU’s democratic model—not only in classrooms but as a lived, co-created practice of citizenship.

Key themes include holistic democratic education, aesthetic and embodied learning, grassroots initiatives, and policy frameworks for educational transformation. The conference brings together educators, researchers, policymakers, NGOs, students, and government officials to share innovations and shape the future of democracy education in Europe.

For more details, and to register click here: https://blog.democrat-horizon.eu/event/education-for-democracy-conference/

Militaries’ expanding remit in climate emergencies: Key findings from a global study
Ronald Kingham & Ashley Moran, 12 August 2025
The article summarizes the Project CASA report "Resilience, Readiness, and Response", which reveals how militaries are increasingly filling critical gaps in disaster response as climate-driven emergencies intensify, despite risks of over-reliance and strain on defence readiness. Drawing on global data and country surveys, it highlights diverse civil-military approaches, from specialized units to joint frameworks, while stressing the urgent need for better cross-national data and coordination. There is need for stronger integration of military and civilian capacities, proactive climate intelligence, and international collaboration to meet the escalating pressures of climate change...

Iran’s water predicament: national, regional and global dimensions
Shabnam Delfani, 8 August 2025
Iran, long defined in headlines by geopolitics, now faces a deeper existential crisis: water bankruptcy, where demand irreversibly outpaces supply, collapsing rivers, aquifers, and agriculture. Decades of mismanagement, climate change, sanctions, and regional tensions converge to erode ecosystems, livelihoods, and the UN-recognized human right to water, with women and rural communities bearing the heaviest burden. The path forward demands urgent reforms—shifting crops, sealing illegal wells, empowering women and youth, and reviving regional water diplomacy—while the global community must treat water security as a universal human right, not a political weapon...
