FOGGS Update: September-October 2023

Upcoming FOGGS Event on UN Day, 24 October
24 October has been globally designated as UN Day, in commemoration of the entering into force of the UN Charter and thus the official establishment of the United Nations Organization on 24 October 1945. FOGGS is hosting a UN Day online discussion at 4 pm Brussels time on Tuesday, 24 October 2023, with senior former UN officials of the Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG). The focus will be on what role the UN can play under the current circumstances of global insecurity because of wars, climate change-related disasters, pandemics, increasing inequalities, and other threats. What adjustments may be needed for the UN to remain relevant for the years to come?

Please register here to attend: https://bit.ly/FOGGSUNDay2023
FOGGS & Partners Event in New York, 15 September 2023
FOGGS, along with other partners hosted a full-day event titled "Inclusive Global Governance for a Peaceful and Resilient World" on 15 September 2023 in New York. The event was held on the sidelines of the UN General Assembly's high-level segment and in view of a number of summits taking place during that period at UN Headquarters in New York. The Programme and other details including the video recording can be found here.
The event included the launch of two reports: Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform edited by Cilene Victor and Georgios Kostakos; An Enhanced Role for the UN in Peace & Human Security edited by Paola Bettelli, Georgios Kostakos and Yoriko Yasukawa.

FOGGS is grateful to the Global Challenges Foundation for its support that enabled these activities. 
High-Level Support for the Global Resilience Council in South Africa
Buyelwa Sonjica, Chairperson of the Inclusive Society Institute's Advisory Council and ex-Cabinet Minister of South Africa, expressed support for the Global Resilience Council championed by FOGGS in an article carried by South African media.

In her article Ms. Sonjica talks about today's complex challenges like climate change and inequality, highlighting the need for better international responses. According to her, Resilience is crucial in addressing these issues, but existing structures like the UN Security Council often fall short. New groups like BRICS seek alternative platforms for their objectives, emphasizing the global importance of resilience. The proposal for a Global Resilience Council (GRC) aims to shift focus from military solutions to addressing structural causes of fragility, fostering a systematic approach to building resilience on a global scale. This initiative could create a more resilient, equitable, and sustainable future. The full article can be found here.

The carnage in Israel and the Gaza Strip seems to have no end, with thousands of fatalities on both sides and a region ready to explode. Who is to blame and how can this end? Read the opinion piece by FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos on Katoikos.world.
Nuclear Facilites as military targets -
the Zaporizhzhia Case
For the first time in history, a nuclear power plant has become a military objective during a war, in Zaporizhzhia, Ukraine. This also highlights the lack of a ratified global agreement or treaty on non-aggression towards nuclear facilities, whose destruction would have a grave impact on civilians. Watch the presentation by nuclear expert Dr Alejandro Zurita and the subsequent discussion organized by FOGGS on 25 September 2023 on behalf of the Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) of former UN system staff. Dr Zurita's presentation can be found on the PRG home page and his paper on the subject can be accessed here.

A Race to the Bottom? Mining the Seabed for Critical Minerals
by Jeffrey Gorham , 13 September 2023
The monumental task of the clean energy transition has touched every corner of the globe, and soon may go even deeper than ever before: the bottom of the ocean. In the race to secure critical minerals needed for a variety of technologies, notably electric vehicle (EV) batteries, some companies have launched exploration missions onto the ocean seabed. Thousands of feet below the surface, a trove of minerals lies untouched, and many want to keep it that way. The battle over the ocean floor came to a head in July of this year, as the International Seabed Authority (ISA), the international organization in charge of deep-sea mining, met to establish a series of rules for mining operations....

Podcast #15 - Multilateralism and the UN in a post-Westphalian world
with Rebecca Shoot, 15 July 2023
Our modern multilateral system is based on a political concept that was born more than 300 years ago and underlies international relations to this day. During the negotiations of the peace of Westphalia, new rules defining the nature and rights of states, notably the principle of non-interference, were established in Europe. This came to be known as the Westphalian world order, an international system emphasizing the role of the sovereign state as the basic unit in the global political arena.
