Dear Friends,

As 2021 is drawing to a close, we at FOGGS hope that you and yours are all in good health and weathering the virtual and actual storms caused by the Covid-19 pandemic, the climate crisis, and the other challenges of our times.
 
In spite of the difficulties, 2021 has been a very productive year for FOGGS. With your support and with the team spirit that defines us, we managed to advance several of our initiatives, including the proposal for a new UN body endowed with Security Council authority but for non-military threats (Global Resilience Council / GRC), the discussion on using military resources for civil defence purposes (The M4CE Project), advancing SDG implementation in India and Greece, and recasting education for the digital, multi-crisis era (the COVIDEA initiative). We are proud of our partners, with whom we launched several of these projects, and grateful towards those who recognized the value of our work and granted us financial assistance, including the Global Challenges Foundation for the GRC Project, the NATO Public Diplomacy Division for the M4CE Project, and Queen Mary University of London for the SDGs in India project.
 
In the new year we are determined to keep up the good fight and make our projects even more broadly known, among decision-makers and the public at large, so that we manage to move the global agenda forward. We plan to continue with the previously mentioned projects, reinforce our efforts to change the narrative of globalization, rethink multilateralism and the UN, and advance global citizenship.
 
We hope to have you with us in the new year too, with your advice on how to do things better, your moral and financial support, to enable us to do the things we can do well, and your actions for the good causes that unite us.
 
With gratitude, solidarity and hope,
Richard Kinley

 COP 26: Moving From the “Glasgow Climate 
Pact” to a Glasgow Climate Impact
The UN Climate Change Conference (COP26) held in Glasgow, Scotland, in November 2021 was a stress test of the capacities of global governance in relation to the deepening climate crisis. In this editorial Richard Kinley, the President of FOGGS, a veteran of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) who was present in Glasgow, takes the temperature of the COP 26 process, reviews its outcomes and shares his advice on the next steps. 
 
A Critical Review of 'Our Common Agenda'
The publication, in September 2021, by the UN Secretary-General of his report Our Common Agenda (OCA) marked the start of a two-year period leading to a proposed multi-stakeholder 'Summit of the Future' in 2023. We welcome the energy and ideas put into this report by the Secretary-General and consider the process leading to the 2023 Summit as crucial for the much-needed renewal of global governance for the 21st Century. At the same time, given the multidimensional crises the world faces, we do not consider the OCA to be bold enough. For more read our paper entitled Global Governance and 'Our Common Agenda': A Critical Review.
Militaries in disaster response 
Military personnel and equipment can play and have already been playing an important role in areas struck by calamities ranging from pandemics, like the current COVID-19 one, to floods, wildfires, hurricanes, earthquakes, industrial accidents, and oil spills. The M4CE Project of FOGGS hosted a panel discussion among experts on this topic on Tuesday, 23 November 2021. You can watch the video recording on the FOGGS YouTube channel.
