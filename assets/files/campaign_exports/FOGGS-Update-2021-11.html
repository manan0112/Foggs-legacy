Militaries in disaster response
Military personnel and equipment can play and have already been playing an important role in areas struck by calamities ranging from pandemics, like the current COVID-19 one, to floods, wildfires, hurricanes, earthquakes, industrial accidents, and oil spills. The M4CE Project of FOGGS is hosting a panel discussion on the role of the "Militaries in responding to Civilian Emergencies (M4CE)", on Tuesday, 23 November 2021. Expert discussants from various countries and organizations have been invited to share good practices and their expertise. You can see the speakers who have confirmed up to now on the event poster that follows.

UN Day 2021 Event - Reviewing  "Our Common Agenda" for a Resilient World
On the occasion of UN Day 2021, FOGGS organised an online discussion on the future of the United Nations on the basis of the ‘Our Common Agenda’ report by UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres. The event included an appraisal of the global governance proposals put forward in the Secretary-General's report by FOGGS Executive Board member Harris Gleckman, on the basis of a Background Paper that can be found here. It was followed by a discussion among FOGGS Executive Board members and guest experts moderated by FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos.
Support for a Global Resilience Council

Following the vivid interest by global governance experts and practitioners, as well as civil society activists in FOGGS' Global Resilience Council (GRC) 
proposal, we are pleased to announce that we have received support from the Global Challenges Foundation (GCF) to promote the proposal, which 
envisages the establishment of a "UN Security Council for tackling non-military threats to human security". Activities foreseen at this stage include outreach to key stakeholders, such as UN member states, UN agencies and 
other intergovernmental organizations, civil society representatives, scientists, parliamentarians and members of the private sector. FOGGS gratefully 
acknowledges the weight and trust lent to its proposal by the GCF. A website dedicated to the GRC will soon be launched so do stay tuned!
