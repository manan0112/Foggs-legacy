FOGGS Update: October 2024
FOGGS Open Consultation Mondays, Call to end Middle East Violence, UN Day Event recording, Democracy Talks, Katoikos articles 
Introducing FOGGS Open Consultation Mondays
Starting on the coming Monday, 4 November 2024, at 3 pm Brussels time, FOGGS will be hosting regular Open Consultation Mondays, convening the world to discuss issues of global importance in 90 minutes each time. We invite all our partners - think tanks, civil society organizations, former UN system staff members, independent experts, students and people from all walks of life to join in our conversations. On 4 November we will be convening the first session on "Israel, the UN and Gaza". For the schedule of Open Consultation Mondays and the topic each time, please check regularly the FOGGS homepage, Upcoming Event(s) section on the top right. 

"Israel, the UN and Gaza"
4 November 2024 | 3:00 - 4:30 pm Brussels 

Register here to join in: https://bit.ly/FOGGS-Open-Consultation-Mondays
Call to end Middle East violence
In the spirit of United Nations Day, 24 October, FOGGS took the initiative to suggest a Call to End the violence in the Middle East Now and for Good. Signed by more than 260 former UN system staff members, the Call asks UN member states and the UN Secretary-General to take "every possible action to put an immediate and permanent end to the war in Gaza and Lebanon." 

The Call with the list of its initial signatories, can be found here: Call-to-end-ME-violence-Final-with-signatures-23-24-Oct-2024.pdf

We welcome further support to the Call, which can now be signed by everyone - former UN system staff or any citizen of our world - through this online form: https://bit.ly/Call_to_End_War_MiddleEast
FOGGS UN Day event recording
The FOGGS UN Day event, "A Raft for the Future: Can the Pact for the Future deliver the Multilateral Titanic?" saw a good discussion with wide participation. The focus was on initial reactions to the Summit of the Future outcome and potential ways of taking it forward, as a whole and its parts referring to Security Council reform and strengthening the Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) in particular.

Stop the MADness of communal hatred and violence
Editorial, 6 October 2024
The anniversary of the 7 October 2023 Hamas attack on Israel finds the Middle East in tatters, with the carnage and destruction threatening global stability. As the war intensifies in Gaza, the West Bank, Lebanon and beyond, deep-seated animosities fuel calls for destruction of "the other side". Urgent action is needed to halt the cycle of violence and promote coexistence and peace in the region.

A war without winners, and a possible way out
by Georgios Kostakos, 28 September 2024
Hezbollah leader Hassan Nasrallah was killed in an Israeli strike, escalating the ongoing Middle East conflict that began with the 7 October 2023 Hamas attack. The violence has resulted in massive casualties on all sides, with Israel's current government pursuing a strategy of total victory despite international concerns. Calls for a ceasefire and a diplomatic resolution are urgent as the situation risks broader regional destabilization.
