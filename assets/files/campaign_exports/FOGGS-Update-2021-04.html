Implementing the SDGs in India
Poverty, Hunger and Gender

Statistical indicators cannot bring about sustainable development unless they are tied to the social context, with existing welfare and rural development schemes brought together under one reference framework. This is a key finding of a collaborative project established with the support of Queen Mary University of London and bringing together the Eco Foundation for Sustainable Alternatives (EFSA), National Law School of India University, O.P. Jindal Global University (JGU) and FOGGS. The project has just published its report titled “Implementing the SDGs in India: Poverty, Hunger and Gender”. You can read the report here and the press release here.
A 'Global Resilience Council': Why and if Yes, How?
Our Brainstorming Note on the proposal for a Global Resilience Council is out!

An online brainstorming session took place on Wednesday, 24 February 2021 under the Chatham House Rule. It was convened by FOGGS to discuss the Foundation’s proposal for the establishment of a “Global Resilience Council”. This Brainstorming Note builds on the points raised during the brainstorming session. It aims at advancing the understanding regarding the need served by and alternative modalities for the creation of a “Global Resilience Council” (GRC) and could lead to actual steps towards bringing it about.

Artists of the Month
Marrugeku: Innovators in Indigenous Dance and Storytelling
Marrukegu is an Australian intercultural dance-theatre company.  They are dedicated to the growth and preservation of Indigenous stories, languages and cultures through the embodied arts of performance-making. We are delighted to present them as FOGGS' artists of the month and look forward to following them more closely.
Financial Times Podcast with Richard Kinley
FOGGS President Richard Kinley was recently the guest at the Financial Times Podcast 'The Rachman Review'. He talked to host Pilita Clark about the achievements and shortcomings of the climate COPs, and what can be done to make them more successful, in view of UNFCCC COP 26 in Glasgow in November. See also Richard's article on the subject at Katoikos.world.
