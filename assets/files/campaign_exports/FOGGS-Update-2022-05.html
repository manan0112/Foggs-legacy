FOGGS Paper on GRC Role in tackling the Climate Crisis
In this discussion paper, FOGGS looks at how the multilateral climate system is failing to effectively respond of the climate crisis, and why there is a need for a Global Resilience Council (GRC). FOGGS Junior Policy and Project Management Advisor Stuart Best highlights the specific mandate gaps in the current UNFCCC-led system, and explains how a GRC would create accountability and enable effective cooperation among a wide variety of stakeholders with a focus on implementation.
Promoting the GRC with the G7
FOGGS has been actively participating in meetings of the Think7 / T7 group of think tanks convened by the German Development Institute (GDI/DIE) and the Global Solutions Initiative under this year's German G7 Presidency. FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos and Executive Board Member Harris Gleckman prepared a policy brief entitled "A Global Resilience Council as a 'UN Security Council' for Human Security Issues like Climate and Pandemics" that is available on the T7 website, under the International Cooperation for the Global Common Good task force of the T7. The authors argue that by supporting the establishment of a Global Resilience Council the G7 would show leadership in re-invigorating the multilateral system. 
Former UN staff address UNSG Guterres
on Ukraine
FOGGS played a central role in the development of an initiative by former UN system staff members, who sent a letter to UN Secretary-General António Guterres imploring him to "intensify [his] personal efforts, deploying all capabilities at [his] disposal and acting upon lessons learnt from previous conflicts, for the cessation of hostilities and conflict resolution through peaceful means" in Ukraine. The letter was sent on 18 April 2022 with 208 initial signatures, to which dozens more were later added. Mr. Guterres actually visited Moscow and Kyiv a few days later and the former UN system staff initiative has been widely acknowledged as a factor that contributed to his decision to take a more active stance now (see relevant articles/interviews in The Guardian 1, The Guardian 2, PassBlue, BBC World News, Foreign Policy). Hopefully a process has been started that will lead from humanitarian ceasefires to proper peace negotiations and a lasting settlement.
FOGGS at the Delphi Economic Forum
FOGGS was a content partner at this year's Delphi Economic Forum that took place in Delphi, Greece from 6 to 9 April 2022. Together with the Global Military Advisory Council on Climate Change (GMACCC), FOGGS sponsored the panel discussion entitled "Towards a Holistic Approach to New Threats of non-military nature". The discussion was moderated by the FOGGS Executive Director Dr. Georgios Kostakos and featured as keynote speakers Dhanasree Jayaram, Assistant Professor, Department of Geopolitics and International Relations, Manipal Academy of Higher Education, India and Olivia Lazard, Visiting Scholar, Carnegie Europe. The recorded video of the discussion can be found here (panel starting around 09:39:30).
