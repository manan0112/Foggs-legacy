FOGGS Update: March 2024
Meeting with Thabo Mbeki Foundation, Zero Draft on the future of global governance, FOGGS@Sparta, UN-FES-FEPS meeting in Brussels, Running out of Diplomats?, Project CASA to host panel discussion, News from our partners, Katoikos articles and more...
Meeting with the Thabo Mbeki Foundation
FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos, along with partner Inclusive Society Institute (ISI) Advisory Council Chairperson Buyelwa Sonjica and CEO Daryl Swanepoel, held consultations in Johannesburg, South Africa, on 6 March 2024, with the Thabo Mbeki Foundation, including CEO Max Boqwana, on possible cooperation regarding global governance initiatives, including the proposal for a UN Global Reliance Council (GRC) initially put forward by FOGGS. The ISI and FOGGS delegation were honoured to be received by and hold an exchange of views with former South African President Thabo Mbeki himself.
Zero Draft on the Future of Global Governance | Online Event held on 1st March, 2024
Watch the video of this online event organised by FOGGS and partners Inclusive Society Institute (ISI) and HumanizaCom on behalf of the Global South Perspectives Network (GSPN) on 1 March 2024. Key points that came up in the discussion are reflected in the op-ed entitled ''UN Summit on the future a catalyst for change" by Nicola Bergsteedt and Daryl Swanepoel of ISI.
FOGGS@Sparta
On 14 March 2024, the office of FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos was inaugurated in Sparta, Greece. It includes a space named 'sparta.komvos', which will host in-person and on-line events on topics related to the mission of FOGGS, as well as on issues of local interest. For more information write to info@foggs.org
FOGGS at "Navigating through the poly-crisis: Towards a global pact for a better future for people and planet"
FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos participated in the workshop entitled "Navigating through the poly-crisis: Towards a global pact for a better future for people and planet", which was convened on 22 March in Brussels by UN Brussels, the Friedrich Ebert Stiftung (FES) and the Foundation for European Progressive Studies (FEPS). The discussion focused on the upcoming UN Summit of the Future, as a vehicle to renew multilateralism and set right inequalities and other ills that have led to lack of effectiveness, lack of leadership and lack of trust in current global governance arrangements among countries and people. For more see here.
"Running out of diplomats?" Upcoming event on the world's Foreign Ministries' staffing problems, 24 April 2024
FOGGS is organising an online event entitled "Running out of diplomats? A discussion on the changing nature of diplomacy and the staffing challenges of the diplomatic sector" on 24 April 2024, at 3:00 pm Brussels time. Recent years have seen staff shortages in Ministries of Foreign Affairs (MFAs) all over the world. While diplomacy has traditionally been perceived as a highly-desirable, prestigious profession that is unlikely to face staff shortages, this perception is increasingly being called into question in light of senior-level staff quitting and an apparent decline in youth interest in joining the corps in the first place. The event will showcase original research conducted by Elisabeth Clemmons and Fotini Zarogianni, with a broad discussion of their findings and recommendations. 

Register here to take part in the event.
