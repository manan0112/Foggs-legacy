FOGGS Update: April-May 2023
C4UN releases “Interim Peopleʼs Pact for the Future”
FOGGS is an integral part of the Coalition for the UN We Need (C4UN) that brings together civil society organizations from around the world to have substantive input into the UN reform process.

On 18 May 2023 the C4UN released an initial compilation of UN reform proposals. It is the result of months of broad-based consultations involving over 1600 civil society leaders worldwide and hundreds of participants in the Global Futures Forum. 
We at FOGGS are delighted to see our proposal for the establishment of a “Global Resilience Council” (GRC) included in the Interim People’s Pact. (on page 7.) Download the report. 
Former UN system officials again call on UNSG to take steps to facilitate a cessation of hostilities in Ukraine
A year after FOGGS coordinated a letter to the UN Secretary-General signed by more than 200 former senior UN officials urging a proactive stance to avoid the worst for the UN and the world as a result of the Russia-Ukraine war, a new letter with concrete suggestions was sent to the UN Secretary-General on 26 April 2023. See both letters on the web page of the informal Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) of former UN system staff that is supported by FOGGS. 
Among other things, the recent letter proposes that the UNSG call on the General Assembly to take steps to facilitate a cessation of hostilities by establishing a Mixed Armistice Commission (MAC – see UNTSO precedent), including representatives of both Russia and Ukraine and chaired by a Special Representative that the Secretary-General would appoint. The letter also proposes the appointment of a “Commission of Experts” tasked with gathering information on all disputed matters to the conflict that would offer recommendations for multilateral action to the General Assembly and eventually the Security Council. Moreover, a "Group of Eminent Women" “could visit the parties to the conflict to advocate for an immediate cessation of hostilities.” 
Announcing the Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform webpage and network 
Together with Co-Convener, Humanizacom, FOGGS is pleased to announce the launch of the Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform. Based on a network of think tanks mainly from the Global South, this forum aspires to influence upcoming decisions on the future of global governance. 
This includes decisions on the reform of the United Nations, so that the states, the communities and the citizens of the Global South no longer feel that they are guests at the mercy of Northern hosts in the halls of global governance, but are equal partners, with their own valid perspectives, and equal right- and obligation-holders in decision-making and action. Meet the members on the dedicated webpage.
The Unbearable Lightness of Being UN Secretary-General
Opinion piece by Georgios Kostakos • PassBlue, 11 May 2023
In a recent article in PassBlue, FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos makes the case that the goal of a UN Secretary-General should not be to try to please everybody, but rather to serve the UN Charter, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the basic principles of moral behaviour that are common across humanity’s many political systems, cultures and religions. Georgios suggests that the UNSG job can be straightforward if the incumbent strives to pursue it that way and sets out six guiding rules, which, if followed, would enable the UN to “set the agenda and lead the action, not just sweep away the mess that the big players make with their fights”. Read the full article.
FOGGS provides inputs to UN Member State consultations on the Summit of the Future (SOTF)
Over the past couple of months, Georgios Kostakos and Harris Gleckman spoke on behalf of FOGGS in a series of online consultations convened by the Permanent Representatives of Germany and Namibia to the UN in New York, in their capacity as co-facilitators of the UN General Assembly process leading to the Summit of the Future in 2024.
Among the topics discussed were “Our Common Agenda Policy Brief 2: Strengthening the International Response to Complex Global Shocks – An Emergency Platform” (March 2023), as well as the
report of the United Nations Secretary-General’s High-Level Advisory Board on Effective Multilateralism (HLAB) (April 2023).

The Summit of the Future is expected to build upon this year’s SDG Summit and breathe new life into the multilateral system so that it can deliver on the promises of the United Nations Charter and the 2030 Agenda. An action-oriented Pact for the Future is expected to be agreed upon by Member States through intergovernmental negotiations as the outcome of the Summit of the Future.
FOGGS Representatives to participate at ACUNS Annual Meeting in Washington
On 21-22 June 2023, several FOGGS colleagues will participate at the Academic Council on the United Nations System (ACUNS) 2023 Annual Meeting entitled “Making, Keeping, and Sustaining Peace” in Washington, D.C. 

FOGGS Executive Director, Georgios Kostakos will speak in roundtable A9 on “Armed Conflict & Crisis in and Around Ukraine: International Reactions and Perspectives”. (22 June 8:00-9:15 am) and will chair roundtable C8 on “International civil servants for life: The Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) and the Russia-Ukraine War (22 June 1:15-2:30 pm) with the participation of PRG members including  Paola Bettelli, Senior Advisor with FOGGS. The latter will also speak along with FOGGS Executive Board member Cilene Victor in roundtable B13 on “Peace Prospects in the Russia-Ukraine War” (22 June 9:30-10:45 am), which will be chaired by FOGGS Advisory Board member Tapio Kanninen. Cilene Victor will also speak in roundtable B10 on “Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform” (21 June 9:30-10:45 am). Read more about the ACUNS 2023 Annual Meeting 
UN Summits 2023 to 2025: What are the Civil Society Proposals? What role for international Geneva?
On 8 June 2023, Greycells - the Association of Former International Civil Servants for Development, Peace Child International, and FOGGS will convene a briefing for the Permanent Missions to the United Nations at Geneva to present key civil society proposals included in the Interim People’s Pact for the Future and their connection to the work done by the UN system entities based in Geneva. The event to be held in person from 13:15 to 14:15 hrs CET is also open to Geneva-based NGOs. For more information, you may contact greycells08@gmail.com or info@foggs.org.

Heliopolis: A Story of Struggle and Hope
by Joao Victor Cruz, 19 April 2023
UNAS (União de Núcleos, Associações dos Moradores de Heliópolis e Região) is the organization that arose from the union and communication between the diverse collectives organized in the territory, not only to defend the right to live in Heliópolis but also to transform it into a place where the rights of all were exercised and guaranteed. Over time the fight for housing became a fight for life, not only in the sense of being alive but also in the sense of being able to live with dignity, understanding that it is the government’s duty and the right of people, especially the poorest, to have a guaranteed quality of life, with health, education, culture, and leisure. A struggle that has lasted for more than 50 years. Read the full article.
Praying for the End of History
by Georgios Kostakos, 8 April 2023
When in the late 1980s and early 1990s, Francis Fukuyama evangelized “the end of history,” he did it in the context of a widely held belief that the collapse of Communism and the victory of Liberal Democracy was a major milestone in human evolution. … Some thirty years later, and not for the first time, history is showing its ugly face again, stating its claim on fame. If the Russian invasion of Ukraine and the subsequent war that continues unabated is not history, then what is it?  The same goes for the heightened tensions in the South China Sea and the Taiwan Strait, increasing inequalities between and within states, intensifying climate impacts, a global financial system that is doing well for itself rather than serving the well-being and resilience of real people and communities, etc. … Instead of “the end of history,” we seem to be witnessing “the revenge of history,” aggressively (re)claiming its own and going forever more. This makes one wonder what the drivers of current history are and what more can be expected if what we already have is not enough?…" Read the full article.
