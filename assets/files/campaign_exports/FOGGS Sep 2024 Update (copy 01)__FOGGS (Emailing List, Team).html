FOGGS at the Summit of the Future
FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos attended a number of events around the UN Summit of the Future (SOTF), as well as part of the Summit itself (22-23 September 2024), in New York City. His impressions of the SOTF and its outcome, the Pact for the Future, can be found in his article entitled "A Pact for Humanity’s Future that Remains Precarious", which was published by To Vima. Among  Georgios' activities while in New York was to present the Global Resilience Council (GRC) proposal at the Social Movements and Organizations Encounter on 20 September 2024.
Register for FOGGS UN Day 2024 event
On the occasion of UN Day, observed annually on 24 October, FOGGS is convening representatives of global think tanks, civil society, academic institutions, diplomatic missions to the UN and foreign ministries, as well as the broader interested public, for a discussion on the Pact for the Future and next steps for strengthening global governance. The first hour will be dedicated to an appraisal of the SOTF, the Pact for the Future and what comes / could come next overall, while the second hour will focus more specifically on UN Security Council reform and on FOGGS' proposal for a Global Resilience Council (GRC) - how they could be taken forward in light of the SOTF outcome and broader developments in the world.

The Nexus Between Narcoterrorism and Jihadism in the West Africa Sahel
by Eleonora Cammarano, 19 September 2024
In her latest article, Eleonora Cammarano examines the intricate connections between narcoterrorism and jihadism in the Sahara and West Sahel regions of Africa. Focusing on Guinea-Bissau's emergence as a key hub in the global drug trade, she explores the impact on local communities and regional stability. The article provides a nuanced overview of how these criminal networks interact and their implications for security in West Africa...

Reflections on the Politics of Race and Language in a French Classroom
by Samuel Showalter, 7 September 2024
This compelling citizen-correspondent article delves into the complexities of language and identity for migrant students in France. Through the lens of an educator, it highlights the challenges faced by students navigating linguistic barriers in a politically charged environment. As discussions shift to rising racial tensions and discrimination, the piece emphasizes the urgent need for greater empathy and inclusivity in a society grappling with questions of fairness and belonging...

BRICS: Navigating a Complex Economic and Political Landscape
by Johnatan Santos , 28 August 2024
In this article, Dr. Johnatan Santos examines the dynamics of the original BRICS—Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa—and their collective role as emerging economic powers challenging established Western dominance. He explores the group's varied economic landscapes and the opportunities for cooperation, particularly in energy and infrastructure. However, he also highlights significant challenges, such as internal competition and the complexities of implementing free trade agreements. As BRICS looks to expand its influence with new members, the article underscores the importance of navigating these differences to create a more inclusive and resilient coalition in a multipolar world...
