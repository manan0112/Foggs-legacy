Armistice Day Call
UNSG and Knotted Gun-UN7930019

Armistice Day is approaching, 11 November, the day that guns fell silent marking the end of the First World War in 1918. "The war to end all wars" did not in fact end all wars... In a Katoikos.world editorial, FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos discusses the continuous relevance of Armistice Day amid ongoing major conflicts, placing respect for human life and dignity and the peaceful resolution of disputes at the centre.

Declaration in support of UN Secretary-General
António Guterres by Former UN Staff
More than 300 former UN system staff, including former heads of UN field operations and offices around the world, signed a declaration supporting UN Secretary-General António Guterres for his objective comments on the situation in the Middle East. PassBlue included a reference to this PRG/FOGGS-facilitated initiative in their recent roundup of activities at the UN. 
See also broader PRG Statement on the Situation in Israel-Palestine.
Further Support for the Global Resilience Council in
South Africa
Daryl Swanepoel, CEO of the Inclusive Society Institute and former member of the South African National Assembly, expressed support for the Global Resilience Council (GRC) championed by FOGGS in a series of articles carried by South African media -- see here.
DEMOCRAT conference in Tallinn, 27-28 February 2024
The EU-funded DEMOCRAT Project, in which FOGGS participates along with nine more partners, will hold its mid-term conference in Tallinn, Estonia, on 27-28 February 2024. It will be an opportunity to present the project findings as of that point and to solicit inputs from the broader community of experts and stakeholders in Education for Democracy -- see more on the DEMOCRAT website.
FOGGS participates in GCF & Partners brainstorming
On 6 and 7 November 2023, the Global Challenges Foundation (GCF) convened in Stockholm, Sweden, an informal brainstorming session on global governance reform. FOGGS was represented by Executive Director Georgios Kostakos and Executive Board member Cilene Victor. The in-depth discussions and identified synergies are expected to enrich the work that all GCF partners are doing to help improve the UN and the broader multilateral system, so that they address more effectively current and emerging threats to humanity and our planet. FOGGS gratefully acknowledges the support provided by GCF for its activities. 
Emerging global geopolitics and the UN  
A UN Day discussion with the PRG
On UN Day 24 October 2023, FOGGS hosted an online discussion with former UN officials of the Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG). The discussion focused on the role the UN can and should play under the current circumstances of global insecurity because of wars, climate change-related disasters, pandemics, increasing inequalities, and other threats. Also discussed were adjustments that need to be made in the UN's structure and processes, so that the world body can fulfil its role today and tomorrow.

Peace and security threats through the lens of the Global South
by Cilene Victor and Georgios Kostakos, 3 November 2023
A recent comprehensive survey conducted by the Global South Perspectives Network, an initiative of the Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability (FOGGS) and the research group Humanitarian Journalism and Media Interventions (HumanizaCom) of the Methodist University of São Paulo, has offered a unique vantage point on the multifaceted landscape of peace and security threats across three significant regions: Latin America and the Caribbean (LATAM), Africa, and the Middle East and North Africa (MENA). With a dataset comprising responses from 531 participants, 512 of them from the target regions (see survey ID map), this survey has provided valuable insights into the nuanced perceptions and challenges related to peace and security in these regions....
