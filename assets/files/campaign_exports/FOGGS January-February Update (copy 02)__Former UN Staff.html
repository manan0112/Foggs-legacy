PRG submits proposals for a New Agenda for Peace 

On 15 January 2023 the FOGGS supported Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) of former UN system staff submitted its “Contribution to the UN Secretary-General’s New Agenda for Peace”. The submission tries to respond to questions/observations that arise from the post-Word War II experience and inter alia proposes:

  • Transferring veto-blocked Security Council agenda items to the UN General Assembly;
  • Encouraging a more proactive stance on the part of the UN Secretary-General for conflict prevention and resolution, on the basis of Article 99 of the UN Charter;
  • Establishing a "Global Resilience Council" to deal with threats to human security;
  • Establishing an Unarmed Observer Corps under the auspices of the UN to protect civilian assets, such as power plants, world heritage sites, etc.;
  • Introducing concepts, strategies and practices that engage and channel the power of people towards conflict resolution, peace-building and sustainment;
  • Investing in education for peace and sustainable development.

Europe Regional Futures Forum - 13 March 2023
The Coalition for the UN We Need (C4UN) partners and global civil society are organizing a series of Regional Futures Forums (RFFs) to bring diverse intergenerational civil society perspectives into preparations for the first annual civil society-led Global Futures Forum (GFF) scheduled for 20-21 March 2023 in New York. The GFF is meant to feed into the intergovernmental process leading to a Summit of the Future (SOTF) in 2024.
The Europe Regional Futures Forum (Europe RFF), covering the entire European continent from West to East, will be held online on Monday, 13 March 2023, from 11 am to 1 pm CET. It will be opened by Maria Fernanda Espinosa, President of the 73rd Session of the UN General Assembly and Co-Chair of the C4UN Steering Committee. The main part of the RFF will consist of seven thematic discussions of 15 minutes each. To participate please register here. 
For those not yet convinced that the establishment of a Global Resilience Council (GRC) would be a good thing, see our 6-minute video in English, with subtitles in French, Spanish, and Russian. We are grateful to the Global Challenges Foundation for their continuing support to promote the GRC.
FOGGS Welcomes Cilene Victor to our Executive Board 
Cilene Victor is a Professor in the Social Communication Program at the Universidade Metodista (UMESP), São Paulo, Brazil, where she is one of the leaders of the research workgroup Humanitarian Journalism and Media Interventions (HumanizaCom), and a Professor at Paulus Communication and Technology College.
She has been doing research and has developed projects focused on the role of communication in the context of climate change, sustainabled evelopment, and disaster risk reduction (DRR). Read Cilene's full profile here.
