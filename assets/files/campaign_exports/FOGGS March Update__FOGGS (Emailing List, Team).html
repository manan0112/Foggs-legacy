Global Futures Forum 2023 held in New York
FOGGS is an integral part of the Coalition for the UN We Need (C4UN) that brings together civil society organizations from around the world to have substantive input
 into the UN reform process. C4UN convened the first annual civil society-led Global Futures Forum (GFF) held on 20-21 March in New York and online.

It followed weeks of online consultations and a series of Regional Futures Forums, including one for Europe (online) on 13 March 2023 that FOGGS co-organized. (See video recording). Discussions focused on seven issue areas/tracks, namely: Global Economic & Financial Architecture, Human Rights & Participation, Development, Global Digital Compact, Environmental Governance, Peace & Security, and UN & Global Governance Innovation. The initial conclusions will shape the proposed “People’s Pact for the Future”, as per the item above. 

FOGGS participated fully in this process, with Executive Director Georgios Kostakos serving as co-facilitator of the Development track, and FOGGS Vice-President Yoriko Yasukawa, Advisory Board member Sudha Reddy and associated Héliopolis, Sao Paulo youth leader João Victor da Cruz de Paula Pinto among the GFF speakers. On the sidelines of the GFF and in the days that followed a team of FOGGS members, notably Executive Board member Harris Gleckman, Senior Advisor Paola Bettelli, and, Georgios Kostakos visited several Permanent Missions to the UN in New York and explained the positions of FOGGS, notably the proposal for a Global Resilience Council. (GRC). Read the GRC Elevator Pitch, watch the short explanatory video, and visit the GRC webpage.

DEMOCRAT project launched
Following a successful proposal under the Horizon Europe Programme of the EU, this 3-year project held its kick-off meeting in Krakow, Poland on 21-22 March. FOGGS is one of ten partners from eight EU countries that form the project consortium, led by the University of Barcelona. The aim is to make project DEMOCRAT's vision of Education for Responsible Democratic Citizenship (EfD) a pivotal means to defend, strengthen and deepen democracy in the EU. EfD does not only refer to knowledge of the formal rules of the democratic political systems. It rather adopts a holistic and humanistic approach, based on the core values of democracy and the centrality of citizens' agency. The project builds partly on the COVID Education Alliance (COVIDEA), an initiative co-led by FOGGS. More on the project in future Updates.

The problems and challenges of climate diplomacy
The Global Citizen:
Podcast No. 12 by Katoikos.world
15 February 2023

Guest speaker, FOGGS President, Richard Kinley, former Deputy Executive Secretary of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) with more than 25 years of experience provided an insider view of this quintessential global forum.
He spoke about his experience in the UNFCCC and its COPs, the biggest challenges he and his colleagues faced during their active years, and how multilateralism itself has evolved since the beginning of the COPs in the mid-90s. Also discussed was whether the international community is doing enough to address climate change, the unfortunate lack of political will in national politics, and the future prospects for climate diplomacy.
