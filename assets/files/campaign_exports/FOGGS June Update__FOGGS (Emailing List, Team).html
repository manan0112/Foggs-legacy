FOGGS Update: June 2023
FOGGS participates in DEMOCRAT partners meeting
The first transnational workshop and the second meeting of the consortium implementing the Horizon Europe project "DEMOCRAT" took place in Amsterdam on 13 and 14 June respectively. FOGGS participated with Georgios Kostakos, Executive Director, and Stephen Downs, senior digital technologies and education expert. The project, which started to be implemented on 1 March 2023, is advancing smoothly, with FOGGS contributing substantively to the conceptual framework and vision for Education for Democracy, as well as by constructing an online Agora for stakeholder engagement and document sharing. You can see more on the project website: https://www.democrat-horizon.eu
FOGGS at ACUNS Annual Meeting 2023
Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform
 
The network of experts and think tanks primarily from the Global South convened by FOGGS and Humanizacom made its first public appearance through a roundtable discussion that was part of the Annual Meeting of the Academic Council on the UN System (ACUNS), held on 21-22 June 2023 in Washington, D.C., and online. In parallel, the network launched a survey on Global South perspectives on global governance reform. Our readers from the Global South are invited to fill out the survey in the language that each feels most comfortable with.
 Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) of former UN system staff

The Peacemaking Reflection Group of former UN system staff, convened by FOGGS, has made its first in-person appearance at the Annual Meeting of the Academic Council on the United Nations System (ACUNS), held this year as per above. FOGGS Executive Director Georgios Kostakos chaired a roundtable discussion on "International civil servants for life: The Peacemaking Reflection Group (PRG) and the Russia-Ukraine War"  with speakers Neal Walker, Gary Slutkin, Michael Heyn, Paola Bettelli and Kerstin Leitner. For more on the PRG, see https://www.foggs.org/prg
Briefing to Geneva Missions and NGOs
Together with Greycells and Peace Child International, FOGGS briefed Geneva based Permanent Missions and NGOs on "UN Summits 2023 to 2025: What are the civil society proposals? What role for International Geneva?"
More details of the briefing, which took place on 8 June 2023, can be found here.
From OUR PARTNERS
Peace Wave 2023
International Peace Bureau and World BEYOND War are planning a second-annual 24-hour peacewave on July 8-9, 2023.

This is a 24-hour-long Zoom meeting featuring live peace actions in the streets and squares of the world, moving around the globe with the sun. This is just prior to an annual NATO meeting, and the peace actions are to oppose all military alliances.
July 9 is also the anniversary of the day in 1955 when Albert Einstein, Bertrand Russell and seven other scientists warned that a choice must be made between war and human survival.

Lithium Fever: Can Electrification be Sustainable?
by Jeffrey Gorham, 23 June 2023
Jeffrey Gorham talks about the significance of lithium mining for global electrification efforts. While lithium powers the transition to electric vehicles, there are significant environmental and social costs associated with its extraction. Gorham explores the need for a multifaceted approach, including investing in public transportation and reducing automobile demand, while acknowledging the efforts of Chile's President, Gabriel Boric, to address mining externalities through nationalization and sustainable extraction methods.

Episode 13 – Is climate change really a crisis of values?
with Finnur Ricart, 31 May 2023
In this thought-provoking episode, we delve into the core of the climate crisis with guest Finnur Ricart, Chairperson of the Icelandic Young Environmentalist Association. We explore how societal values centered around growth, expansion, individualism, and consumption have led to the environmental breakdown. Finnur shares his firsthand experience as an Icelandic UN Youth Delegate on Climate Change and highlights the impact of youth voices in international diplomatic processes. There is a need to rethink our relationship with life and nature to address the challenges of climate change effectively. Tune in to gain valuable insights and perspectives on this pressing issue.
