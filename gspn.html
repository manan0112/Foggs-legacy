Global South Perspectives Network (GSPN)

A Global South-led network advancing inclusive multilateralism and fairer global governance.

Overview

The Global South Perspectives Network (GSPN) is a collaborative network of think tanks and individual experts working to promote more inclusive multilateralism and fairer global governance policies from the perspective of the Global South.

GSPN serves as a platform for dialogue, research, and collaboration among policy researchers and practitioners engaged in international policy debates. Through exchanges, joint initiatives, and participation in global policy discussions, GSPN amplifies Global South voices and contributes to a more balanced international order.

The network is convened jointly by the Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability, the Inclusive Society Institute, and the HumanizaCom Research Group.

Vision and Objectives

Vision

To be a leading network of think tanks and experts advocating for a more inclusive multilateral system and policies that reflect the perspectives and priorities of the Global South.

Objectives

  • Provide a platform for exchanging news, research, and ideas on multilateralism and international policy coordination.
  • Undertake and disseminate research on global governance and international cooperation.
  • Advocate for a more inclusive international order with fairer policies.
  • Expand and strengthen the network of participating think tanks and experts.

Background and Context

The Global South Perspectives Network emerged amid successive global crises following the end of the Cold War—intensifying climate change, the 2008 financial crisis, the COVID-19 pandemic, food and water insecurity, and ongoing conflicts. Geopolitical tensions among major powers continue to shape the international system.

Global governance debates have long been dominated by industrialized countries, but Global South actors are increasingly articulating their own perspectives on cooperation and policy. GSPN provides a platform for these voices, fostering dialogue and collaboration on the future of multilateralism and UN reform.

The network aims to ensure that countries and societies from the Global South are equal partners in shaping international decision-making and action.

How the Network Works

Quarterly Policy Dialogues

Quarterly dialogues address policy issues identified by participants, with guiding questions circulated in advance to shape contributions.

Information Exchange

Members share events from their institutions and flag key international policy processes—such as UN conferences and global consultations—to coordinate potential participation.

Collaborative Initiatives

Participants explore opportunities for joint research, policy dialogue, and engagement in international processes at national, regional, or global levels.

Co-Convening Organisations

HumanizaCom Research Group

Hosts research, technical, and outreach projects at the Methodist University of São Paulo, focusing on communication and journalism related to humanitarian issues, peace journalism, media interventions, and risk communication, in collaboration with national and international partners.

Inclusive Society Institute

An autonomous research and policy organization in South Africa dedicated to strengthening multi-party democracy and advancing social justice, cohesion, and economic development through research, dialogue, and collaboration.

Foundation for Global Governance and Sustainability

A Brussels-based think-and-do tank (est. 2013) working on global governance reform and sustainable globalization, acting as research centre, discussion forum, and advocacy platform, with a focus since 2020 on strengthening the UN and global governance for human security, resilience, and peace.

Events and Dialogues

  • Inclusive Global Governance for a Peaceful and Resilient World — 15 September 2023 | New York, USA

    Full-day event on the sidelines of the UN General Assembly high-level segment, gathering experts and stakeholders to discuss inclusive global governance reform and launch two reports: “Global South Perspectives on Global Governance Reform” and “An Enhanced Role for the UN in Peace & Human Security.”

  • Zero Draft on the Future of Global Governance — 1 March 2024 | Online Webinar

    Examined the Zero Draft of the Pact for the Future ahead of the UN Summit of the Future, with speakers from policy institutions and academia exploring collective action, inclusive policymaking, and strengthened multilateral cooperation from a Global South perspective.

  • The Role of Middle Powers — Africa Consultative Meeting — 25–26 March 2025 | Cape Town

    At the Inclusive Society Institute’s consultative meeting, GSPN participation focused on the role of middle powers in rebalancing global governance and revitalizing multilateralism and the UN.
