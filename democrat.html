DEMOCRAT | Education for Democracy

Education for Responsible Democratic Citizenship

Overview

DEMOCRAT – Education for Democracy was a research and innovation project aimed at strengthening liberal democracy in the European Union by redesigning and implementing context-sensitive Education for Responsible Democratic Citizenship (EfD).

The project advanced curricula, learning methods, and participatory approaches that enable students, teachers, and communities to become active democratic agents. It promoted formal and informal learning through programmes for pupils, teachers, and social educators.

DEMOCRAT built a multidisciplinary competence framework for responsible democratic citizenship and translated it into adaptable curricula and practical learning tools, piloted across several European countries with schools, municipalities, civil society organisations, public authorities, and education professionals. The project was funded under the European Union’s Horizon Europe Research and Innovation programme.

Background and Context

DEMOCRAT responded to converging pressures on democratic governance in Europe: climate change, biodiversity loss, pandemics, economic crises, social inequalities, and rapid digital transformation. These dynamics intensified debates about the resilience of liberal democracy and highlighted declining trust, misinformation, extremism, and reduced civic engagement.

Education emerged as a central lever. European policy frameworks emphasise education’s role in democratic values, social inclusion, human rights, and global citizenship, grounded in the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights, the Treaty on European Union, and the United Nations 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

Education for Responsible Democratic Citizenship adopts a holistic perspective: building democratic competences—knowledge, skills, and attitudes—to help people navigate complexity and participate constructively in democratic life, reinforcing democratic resilience and sustainable development.

Project Activities

Research and Conceptual Frameworks

Developed a shared understanding of democracy, participation, and responsible citizenship, including analysis of curricula and strategies across Europe. Examined links between educational inequalities, socio-economic background, and political participation, including misinformation and digital learning environments. Produced the Responsible Democratic Citizenship competence framework that underpinned new EfD curricula and learning approaches.

Curriculum Development and Educational Tools

Designed flexible curricula and learning approaches for students, for teachers and social educators. Emphasised learner-centred methods such as transformative education, action learning, and challenge-based learning to enable experiential, participatory democratic practice. Compiled a toolbox with the competence framework, curricula, guidance, learning units, assessment tools, and policy recommendations.

Pilot Projects and Living Labs

Implemented Local Pilot Projects (LPPs) in Estonia, Finland, Germany, Ireland, Spain, and Poland. National and transnational living labs convened schools, municipalities, educators, parents, civil society organisations, and public authorities to co-create educational practices and share learning across countries.

Agora Platform

The Agora was an interactive digital platform supporting collaboration, dialogue, and knowledge exchange among education-for-democracy stakeholders. It hosted deliverables, multilingual resources, community tools (blogs, podcasts, discussion forums), and provided access to the project toolbox.

The platform supported national and transnational collaboration in English and participating countries’ languages. Built on Nextcloud for secure data management and privacy compliance, it included accessibility features to remain usable beyond the project lifespan.

Events and Dialogues

  • Strengthening Democracy Through Education — Conference — 27–28 February 2024 | Tallinn University

    Explored conceptual and practical perspectives on responsible democratic citizenship through education, bringing together researchers, educators, policymakers, and practitioners. Sessions covered democratic competences, citizenship education, socio-economic factors in civic engagement, innovative approaches, curriculum development, and school-community collaborations, with networking and collaboration across related European projects.

  • DEMOCRAT Final Conference — 20–21 November 2025 | Barcelona

    Presented project outcomes and examined how education can strengthen democratic resilience in Europe. Brought together educators, researchers, civil society, and policymakers to discuss integrating democratic learning into education systems, share participatory approaches, and connect grassroots democratic education initiatives with policy strategies for scaling across Europe.
