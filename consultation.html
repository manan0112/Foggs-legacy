FOGGS Open Consultation Mondays

Monthly online consultations fostering open dialogue on global governance challenges.

Overview

FOGGS Open Consultation Mondays provided a forum for experts, practitioners, and interested participants to discuss emerging geopolitical developments, multilateral cooperation, and the evolving architecture of international governance—encouraging reflection on peace, sustainability, and international cooperation.

Background and Context

Launched amid rising geopolitical tension, climate crises, economic instability, and technological disruption, the series responded to calls for reform of global governance structures. Sessions created space for structured dialogue on shifts affecting the international system, from multilateral reform to digital governance, development finance, and climate cooperation.

The Initiative

Open Consultation Mondays functioned as an online dialogue platform bringing together experts, policymakers, researchers, and civil society representatives. Each session focused on a theme tied to global governance or multilateral institutions, prioritizing open participation and active discussion over formal presentations.

Activities

The series covered themes such as UN reform, geopolitical shifts, roles of middle powers and civil society, climate governance, financing for development, and the influence of narratives and leadership on international politics. Discussions fostered exchange across academic, policy, and civil society communities to surface pathways for stronger multilateral cooperation.

  • #2: Shifting Geopolitics and its Impact on Global Governance and the UN — BRICS expansion, regional conflicts, and economic challenges reshaping governance.
  • #3: Building a Culture of Peace at a Time of Global Turmoil — rising conflicts and multilateral challenges for peace.
  • #4: How Multilateralism Can More Effectively Enable Action on Climate Change — improving cooperation for mitigation and adaptation.
  • #5: Decolonizing the UN — addressing historical legacies and representation in UN governance.
  • #6: Leadership as Content Creation – Where Have Ethics and Substance Gone? — social media-driven communication, governance, and public trust.
  • #7: The Role of Middle Powers and Citizens in Balancing the International System — middle powers and citizen diplomacy shaping global dynamics.
  • #8: A New Narrative for Peace and Resilience — shared narratives and values for cooperation and resilience.
  • #9: The 4th International Conference on Financing for Development — development finance challenges and equitable economic systems.
  • #10: State-Led Global Public Goods Provision and the Potential UN Regulatory Role — international infrastructure initiatives and implications for multilateral governance.
  • #11: Shaping Global Governance through Middle Power Capabilities — navigating geopolitical polarization and contributing to norm-setting.
  • #12: Impressions from the 80th UN General Assembly Session Opening — reform debates on priorities and representation.
  • #13: Global South Perspectives on the UN — how rising Global South influence is reshaping representation and reform debates.
  • #14: Dissecting China’s Global Governance Initiative — examining whether it expands influence or responds to gaps in current systems.
